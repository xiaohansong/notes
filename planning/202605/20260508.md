## Pre-session decision (before walking up)

Decide before warm-ups: **345×3** (PR attempt) or **335×3** (confirm bank). Default = 345 attempt with bail rule. If sleep was bad, RDL Tue felt grindy, or warm-up squats feel off → drop to 335×3 confirm and don't apologize.

Do NOT walk in and let the bar tell you. Pick now.

## 1. Back Squat — Top Heavy Triple

```
1 working triple
3 reps @ 345#  (target RPE 9)
```

**Bail rules — preset, not in-the-moment:**
- Rep 1 RPE 9+ → drop the bar after rep 1, re-attempt at 335 after 4 min rest. A clean 335×3 is a productive day; a missed 3rd at 345 is wasted CNS.
- Rep 2 hesitates or grinds → bail. Hesitation = bail rule applies. Don't try rep 3 to "see if it's there."
- Reps 1 and 2 fly → finish rep 3 with full intent. Clean rep 3 = the PR.

Belt and shoes on. 4–6 min rest after the last warm-up single. 335×3 already banked 5/1. Today is genuinely the next progression — not chasing, not redoing.

**Decision rule (next heavy day):** clean 345×3 (RPE ≤ 9) → target 350#×3. Grindy (RPE 9.5) → repeat 345 next time. Bailed → repeat 345 with proper rest, don't retreat to 335.

## 2. Back Squat — Hypertrophy Back-off

```
4 sets, ~3 min rest
5 reps @ 295#  (78%)
```

Lighter than Mon's 315# 5×5 on purpose — you already got the volume work Monday. Quality-rep back-offs, not a second hard session. Same brace, same depth, smooth tempo. If 295# moves like air across all 4 sets, next heavy day's back-off can go to 300#.

If the heavy triple bailed or grinded badly, drop to 3 sets here and call it a day — don't compound the CNS hit.

## 3. Box Step-up — Single-Leg Hypertrophy

```
4 sets, every 2:30
8 reps each side @ 30–35# DBs, knee-height box (~18–20")
```

Fresh single-leg variant — first step-up session in the visible 28-day window. Different stimulus than BSS (dynamic ascend vs. static rear-foot-elevated), and lower spinal load than any squat variant — perfect after a heavy triple. Drive through the heel of the working leg, full extension at the top, controlled descent (no plyo bounce). Don't push off the trailing foot — that's the cheat. If 30# feels easy, bump to 35# next time.

If the heavy triple genuinely cooked you, you can skip this. But the step-up is a meaningful hypertrophy hit, not filler — only skip if you'd skip BSS too.

## 4. GHD Back Extension (or Good Morning) — Anti-flexion Core

```
3 sets × 10 reps, NFT
BW (or +10# plate hugged to chest if BW is RPE 5)
```

Posterior, light, anti-flexion — Balance has this bucket at 7 sets in the window and flagged under at ~3/wk target. Different stimulus from squat (no spinal compression load), and complements the heavy triple recovery curve. Hinge at the hips, neutral spine, squeeze glutes at top — no overextension. If you don't have a GHD, sub good morning @ empty bar or 65# for 10 reps.
