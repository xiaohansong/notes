# Fri 5/8 — Squat 3s (Heavy Day) — 345# Top Triple Attempt

> **Week frame:** Heavy squat day. Open at 345×3 with the bail rule preset — no ladder up like 5/1. Hypertrophy backoffs after.

## Pre-session decision (before walking up)

Decide before warmups: are you opening at **345×3** (PR attempt) or **335×3** (confirm bank)? Default = 345 attempt with bail rule. If sleep was bad, RDL Tue felt grindy, or warmup squats feel off — drop to 335×3 confirm and don't apologize.

Do NOT walk in and let the bar tell you. Pick now.

## 1. Back Squat — Top Heavy Triple

**1 working triple:** `3 reps @ 345#` (target RPE 9)

**Bail rules — preset, not in-the-moment:**
- Rep 1 feels RPE 9+ → drop the bar after rep 1 and re-attempt at 335 after 4 min rest. A clean 335×3 today is a productive day; a missed 3rd at 345 is wasted CNS.
- Rep 2 hesitates or grinds → bail. Hesitation = bail rule applies. Don't try rep 3 to "see if it's there."
- Rep 1 and 2 fly → finish rep 3 with full intent. If rep 3 lands clean, that's the PR.

**Notes:** Belt and shoes on. 4–6 min rest after the last warm-up single. 335×3 already banked 5/1. Today is genuinely the next progression — not chasing, not redoing.

**Decision rule (next heavy day):** if 345×3 lands clean (RPE ≤ 9) → target 350#×3. If it grinds (RPE 9.5) → repeat 345 next time. If it bails → repeat 345 with proper rest next time, don't retreat to 335.

## 2. Back Squat — Hypertrophy Back-off

**4 sets, ~3 min rest:** `5 reps @ 295#` (78%)

**Notes:** Lighter than Mon's 315# 5×5 on purpose — you already got the volume work Monday. These are quality-rep back-offs, not a second hard session. Same brace, same depth, smooth tempo. If 295# moves like air across all 4 sets, next heavy day's back-off can go to 300#.

If the heavy triple bailed or grinded badly, drop to 3 sets here and call it a day — don't compound CNS hit.

## 3. Box Step-up — Single-Leg Hypertrophy

**4 sets, every 2:30:** `8 reps each side @ 30–35# DBs, knee-height box (~18–20")`

**Notes:** Fresh single-leg variant — first step-up session in the visible 28-day window. Different stimulus than BSS (dynamic ascend vs. static rear-foot-elevated), and lower spinal load than any squat variant — perfect after a heavy triple. Drive through the heel of the working leg, full extension at the top, controlled descent (no plyo bounce). Don't push off the trailing foot — that's the cheat. If 30# feels easy, bump to 35# next time.

If heavy triple genuinely cooked you, you can skip this. But the step-up is a meaningful hypertrophy hit, not filler — only skip if you'd skip BSS too.
